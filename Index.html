Why this plan works

Three levers drive your goals: enough hard sets per muscle, progressive overload (the weight/reps creeping up week to week — that's what the tracker is for), and exercise selection that spares the knee.

  • Volume target: 10–20 hard sets per muscle per week, split across 2+ sessions, is the best-supported range for muscle growth — with diminishing returns above that. Glutes and chest are programmed at the upper end since they're your priorities.
  • Frequency: hitting glutes and chest twice weekly (not once) lets you accumulate that volume with quality sets.
  • Effort: take working sets to within ~1–3 reps of failure. Warm-ups don't count toward volume.

Schoenfeld, Ogborn & Krieger (2017), J Sports Sci; Pelland et al. (2024) dose-response meta-regression.

Glutes without trashing the knee

The big finding: hip thrusts build the glutes as well as squats — with the highest glute activation of any lift — while keeping the knee at a fixed, safe angle. That's ideal for you.

  • Lead with hip-dominant work: hip thrusts, glute bridges, kettlebell/Romanian deadlifts, cable pull-throughs and swings load the glutes through the hips, not deep knee bend.
  • Train the side glutes: hip abduction strengthens the gluteus medius, which keeps the knee from caving inward — directly protective for your joint.
  • Limit deep knee flexion under load: go easy on deep squats and forward lunges; favor reverse-style and partial ranges, and keep leg-press depth comfortable.

Plotkin et al. (2023), Front Physiol; Contreras et al. (2015), J Appl Biomech.

Chest & toned arms

For chest, alternate a press (overload) with a fly (stretch under load) twice a week. "Toned" arms = the same muscle worked through a full range — higher reps just feel different, they don't create a separate "tone" effect, so train biceps/triceps with real, progressive load.

The 6-pack reality

Visible abs come from two things together: trained, thicker ab muscles and low enough body fat to see them. So this plan trains abs with resistance (weighted crunches, leg raises, anti-rotation), but the "seeing them" part is mostly your nutrition and overall body fat — abs you can't out-crunch a calorie surplus.

Sunny H&F evidence review (2026); BodySpec (2026).

Sunday dance & recovery

Your dance class is your cardio + conditioning day — it supports the fat-loss side of the abs goal without extra joint pounding. Wednesday and Saturday are kept light on purpose so your glutes and chest actually recover and grow.

On your knee: this plan minimizes knee load, but a Baker's cyst usually points to something happening inside the joint. Run this past your physio or doctor, keep doing your prescribed rehab, and stop any movement that creates sharp or swelling-type pain rather than normal muscle effort.
